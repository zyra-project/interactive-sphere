The mission

"It is one of the most powerful tools ever built for communicating environmental science. But you have to be in the room."

Science On a Sphere is NOAA's six-foot suspended globe — a room-sized installation projecting planetary data across its surface. Hurricanes spin in real time, sea ice retreats season by season, ocean temperatures shift across decades. It has lived in museums, universities, and visitor centers since 2000.

Most people will never stand in front of one. Geography, mobility, access, and cost all create gaps between NOAA's data and the public it was collected to serve.

TerraViz closes those gaps. Same data — no museum required.
