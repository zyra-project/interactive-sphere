The mission

It is one of the most powerful tools ever built for communicating environmental science. But you have to be in the room.

Science On a Sphere is NOAA's six-foot suspended globe — a room-sized installation projecting planetary data across its surface. Hurricanes spin in real time, sea ice retreats season by season, ocean temperatures shift across decades. It has lived in museums, universities, and visitor centers since 2000. TerraViz brings the same view to any device, and lets anyone publish alongside NOAA's data.

Most people will never stand in front of one. Geography, mobility, access, and cost all create gaps between NOAA's data and the public it was collected to serve.

The same gaps face the research groups, universities, science museums, planetariums, and visitor centers with visualizations of their own to share — and want to keep their data on their own infrastructure. The path to public reach has historically run through a museum partnership that may never come. TerraViz offers a four-tier on-ramp instead: publish a dataset to the live catalog in minutes, mirror that catalog locally in hours, host a full peer in days, or implement your own node against the published spec in weeks.

Viewable on any device. Publishable from any node — your hardware, your domain, one publish reaching every federated peer.

Same data, no museum required. Same publishers, no platform required.
